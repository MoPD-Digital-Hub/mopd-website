UN Secretary-General António Guterres Praises Ethiopia's Commitment to Climate & Food Policy

UN Secretary-General António Guterres praised Ethiopia's commitment in aligning food policy with climate and environmental goals.

In his video message to the UN Food Systems Summit +4 Stocktaking Moment in Addis Ababa, Secretary-General António Guterres emphasized that food systems encompass nutrition, justice, health, education, decent jobs, and the right to a better future.

He acknowledged significant progress since the first summit: two-thirds of countries have integrated food systems into national development plans, over a hundred aligned with climate and biodiversity goals, and 155 countries are advancing national conveners to engage business, civil society, Indigenous peoples, farmers, and youth.

Guterres stressed that despite these strides, progress is not fast or fair enough. Hunger is rising, trade shocks are pushing food prices higher, and a staggering one-third of the world's population cannot access a healthy diet.

He declared that the crisis is not one of scarcity, but of justice, equity, and climate — and specifically lauded Ethiopia's commitment in aligning food policy with climate and environmental goals.
