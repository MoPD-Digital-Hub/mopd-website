Supervision Conducted on Ethiopian Electronic Public Procurement System

A supervision was conducted to assess the implementation status of the Ethiopian Electronic Public Procurement system, coordinated by the Ministry of Planning and Development with participation from various public institutions.

The objective was to evaluate implementation progress, identify good practices and challenges, and highlight reforms needed at policy and administrative levels to ensure the system's effectiveness.

The team visited system infrastructure, observed service delivery procedures, and reviewed support provided to suppliers and the public sector. Leaders from the Public Procurement and Property Authority responded to technical inquiries on integration, challenges, and security risks.

A consolidated report on the system's status, lessons learned, and issues requiring government attention will be prepared based on feedback from stakeholders including public bodies and procurement suppliers.
